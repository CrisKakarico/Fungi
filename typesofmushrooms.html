Types of Mushrooms

Chanterelle Mushroom

Cantharellus cibarius

Chanterelle mushroom is a bright orange or yellow fungus with a fruity aroma, nutty flavor, and slightly chewy texture. Chanterelles are highly prized by food enthusiasts for their versatility, and to be a source of vitamins and minerals, as well as antioxidants and anti-inflammatory compounds. Chanterelles are in season from late summer to early fall and can be found fresh, dried, or canned year-round. They add flavor to many dishes and are worth trying for their taste and potential health benefits. It are common in Eurasia, North, Central America and Africa, and tend to grow in clusters in mossy coniferous forests.

Porcini Mushroom

Boletus edulis

Porcini mushrooms are a type of edible fungus with a meaty texture and rich, nutty flavor. They are popular in Italian and French cooking and can be used in a variety of dishes, from soups and stews to pasta dishes and risottos. Porcini mushrooms are a good source of protein, fiber, vitamins and minerals, and may have anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. They are highly prized for their flavor and versatility in cooking. Porcini mushrooms grow in small clusters near trees in forests and can be found in the Northern Hemisphere across parts of Europe.

Shiitake Mushroom

Lentinula edodes

The Shiitake mushroom is a versatile mushroom that has a slightly smoky and earthy flavor. It has a chewy texture and is often used in Asian dishes, such as stir-fry and soup. They are available fresh, dried, or canned, and are a good source of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin D, iron, and zinc. Shiitake mushrooms also contain compounds that may help boost the immune system and reduce inflammation. Native to East Asia, around 83% of the World's Shiitakes are produced in Japan.

Button Mushroom

Agaricus bisporus

Button mushroom also referred to as white mushroom, is a type of mushroom that grown extensively. This mushroom variety features a smooth, white cap and a tender, edible stem. They are a source of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants and can be eaten raw or cooked in many dishes, including salads, pasta, and soups. Button mushrooms are available year-round in grocery stores and are a affordable ingredient to cooking. They are found in fields and grasslands all over the world, but they are also highly cultivated.

Portobello Mushroom

Agaricus bisporus

Portobello mushrooms are a large, meaty type of edible fungus with a firm texture and rich, earthy flavor. They are a mature form of the common white button mushroom and are popular in vegetarian and vegan dishes as a meat substitute. These mushrooms are low in calories and can be grilled, roasted, saut√©ed, or baked. They are available year-round in grocery stores and can be enjoyed in a variety of dishes. It can be found in the grass, near manure piles, and on leaf litter near conifers, especially in the northern hemisphere.

Morel Mushroom

Morchella esculenta

Morel mushrooms are a highly prized type of edible mushroom known for their unique honeycomb-like appearance and earthy, nutty flavor. They are typically found in forests and woodlands during the spring months and are a favorite among chefs. Morels are low in calories and high in protein, fiber, and essential vitamins and minerals, and can be cooked in a variety of ways to enhance their delicious flavor and chewy texture. Morels are most commonly found in woodlands or woody edges.
